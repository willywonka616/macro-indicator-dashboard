## Why the roll-problems rows didn't ship live

Four live CI round trips (documented in full in
`docs/review/2026-07-23b-verification.md` and STATUS.md §30) found:
- MSPD's `mspd_table_3_market` individual-security rows sum to **~2.9x**
  the table's own "Total Marketable" row for the same date — a real,
  unexplained discrepancy.
- The leading hypothesis (Treasury reopening a CUSIP across multiple
  rows, causing double-counting) was tested directly by deduplicating —
  the sum **did not change at all**, disconfirming that hypothesis.
- The maturing-within-12-months share computed from that data (~11%) is
  implausibly low against the well-known fact that Treasury Bills alone
  (~$6-7T, all maturing within a year) should push the true share well
  above that.

Per the task's own instruction to cap iteration and document the
residual rather than chase it indefinitely, both roll-problems rows stay
manual, each carrying this investigation summary inline in
`data/manual.json` for a future session to pick up.
